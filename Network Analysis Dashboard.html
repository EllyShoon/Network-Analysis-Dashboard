Network Structural Figures
Figure 1
Degree Distribution
How many companies have each number of connections. 75.7% of all 5,042 companies have exactly 2 connections — they sit in the middle of an ownership chain with one parent and one subsidiary. Only a tiny fraction (like KT Sports at 13) act as real hubs.
Figure 2
Top 5 Brokers — Betweenness Centrality
Betweenness measures how often a company sits on the shortest path between two other companies. A company with high betweenness acts as a bridge — information and ownership links must pass through it. Removing it would disconnect parts of the network.
Figure 3
Top 5 — Eigenvector Centrality (KT Cluster)
Eigenvector measures influence through well-connected neighbours — being connected to powerful companies makes you powerful. All top 10 are from the KT Group, forming a tight, internally influential cluster.
Figure 4
Top 5 — Closeness Centrality
Closeness measures how quickly a company can reach every other company in the network. The values are very tightly packed (all between 0.0236–0.0238) because the network is so large. The chart zooms in to show the real differences clearly.
Figure 5
Network Metrics — Sparsity & Clustering
Both density and clustering are near zero, which means companies are mostly in long chains rather than tight groups. This creates many structural holes — gaps between clusters that well-placed companies can bridge for strategic advantage (Burt, 2004).

Data Completeness (5,042 companies)
